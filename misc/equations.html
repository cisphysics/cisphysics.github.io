Vector Operations
Vector Magnitude \begin{split} |\vec{u}| &= \sqrt{u_x^2+u_y^2}\\ &= \sqrt{\textstyle\sum^{N}_{i=1}u_i^2} \end{split}

To find the magnitude, sum the squares of the components then take the square root. Basically Pythagorean Theorem.


Vector Addition \begin{split} \vec{u}+\vec{v} &= (u_x+v_x)\hat{i}+(u_y+v_y)\hat{j}\\ &= \textstyle\sum^{N}_{i=1}(u_i+v_i)\hat{e_i} \end{split}

The components of the resultant vector are the sum of the corresponding components of its constituents. The second equation looks scary, but its just the general form for summing any two N-dimensional vectors.


Scalar Product / Dot Product \begin{split} \vec{u}\cdot\vec{v} &= u_xv_x+u_yv_y\\ &= |\vec{u}||\vec{v}|\hbox{cos}(\theta)\\ &= \textstyle\sum^{N}_{i=1}u_iv_i \end{split}

The scalar or dot product simply means how much of $\vec{u}$ is pointing in the $\vec{v}$ direction times the magnitude of $\vec{u}$ or vice versa. It comes in two forms. The first form is the sum of the product of the components and the second is the product of the magnitudes multiplied by the cosine of the angle formed between the two vectors.


Vector Product / Cross Product \begin{split} \vec{u}\times\vec{v} &= (u_yv_z-u_zv_y)\hat{i}+(u_xv_z-u_zv_x)\hat{j}+(u_xv_y-u_yv_x)\hat{k}\\ &= \begin{vmatrix} \hat{i} & \hat{j} & \hat{k}\\ u_x & u_y & u_z\\ v_x & v_y & v_z \end{vmatrix}\\\\ |\vec{u}\times\vec{v}| &= |\vec{u}||\vec{v}|\hbox{sin}(\theta)\\\\ A\times B &= -B\times A \end{split}

I can't give this example in two dimensions since cross products can only exist in three or seven dimensions (more info). A vector made by crossing two vectors points in the direction perpendicular to the plane created by the two vectors. It is a noncommutative operator; $A\times B\neq B\times A$.


Kinematics
Displacement, Velocity, and Acceleration Relations \begin{split} v(t) &= \frac{\hbox{d}}{\hbox{d}t}\ x(t)\\ a(t) &= \frac{\hbox{d}^2}{\hbox{d}t^2}\ x(t) \end{split}

Velocity is the rate of change of displacement over time, and acceleration is the rate of change of velocity over time (or the rate of change of the rate of change of displacement over time over time).


Simple Equations of Motion $$v=v_0+at$$ $$x=x_0+v_0t+\frac{1}{2}at^2$$ $$x=\frac{1}{2}(v_0+v)t$$ $$2ax=v^2-v_0^2$$

These equations only work if acceleration is constant.
