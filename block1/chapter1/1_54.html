Problem 1.54

An acre, a unit of land measurement still in wide use, has a length of one furlong ($\frac{1}{8}$ mi) and a width of one-tenth of its length. (a) How many acres are in a square mile? (b) How many square feet are in an acre? (c) An acre-foot is the volume of water that would cover 1 acre of flat land to a depth of 1 foot. How many gallons are in 1 acre-foot?

Solution

We are given that one acre is $\frac{1}{8}$ miles by $\frac{1}{80}$ miles. Therefore, it is $\frac{1}{640}$ square miles. To find the number of acres in a square mile, we do the following:


\begin{split} \frac{1}{640}\hbox{ mi}^2 &= 1\hbox{ acre} \end{split}

Multiplying both sides by 640, we get:

\begin{split} 1\hbox{ mi}^2 &= 640\hbox{ acre} \end{split}

There are 5280 ft in 1 mile. To find how many square feet there are in an acre, we substitute feet into square miles:

\begin{split} 1\hbox{(5280 ft)}^2 &= 640\hbox{ acre}\\ 27878400\hbox{ ft}^2 &= 640\hbox{ acre}\\ 43560\hbox{ ft}^2 &= 1\hbox{ acre} \end{split}
