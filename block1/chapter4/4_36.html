Problem 4.35

You have just landed on Planet X. You take out a 100-g ball, release it from rest from a height of 10.0 m, and measure that it takes 2.2 s to reach the ground. You can ignore any force on the ball from the atmosphere of the planet. How much does the 100-g ball weigh on the surface of Planet X?

Solution

Steps to solving the problem:

  1. Find acceleration caused by Planet X's gravity
  2. Find weight

We know that the ball had an initial velocity of 0 ms$^{-1}$, and it took 2.2 s for it to travel 10.0 m with constant acceleration. To find the acceleration, we can use the following equation:

$$s=ut+\frac{1}{2}at^2$$

Plugging in our known values, we get:

$$10=(0)t+\frac{1}{2}a(2.2)^2$$ $$10=2.42a$$ $$a=4.13\ \hbox{ms}^{-2}$$

To find the weight of the ball on Planet X, we can use the equation $F_W=mg$, where $F_W$ is in Newtons, $m$ is in kilograms, and $g$ is in meters per second squared. Hence, substituting in the values, we get:

\begin{split} F_{W, \hbox{ball}} & = mg\\ & = (0.1)(4.13)\\ & = 0.413\ \hbox{N} \end{split}
