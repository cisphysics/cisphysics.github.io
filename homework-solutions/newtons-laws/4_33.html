Problem 4.33

A 4.80-kg bucket of water is accelerated up by a cord of negligible mass whose breaking strength is 75.0 N. If the bucket starts from rest, what is the minimum time required to raise the bucket a vertical distance of 12.0 m without breaking the cord?

Solution

Steps to solving the problem:

  1. Find the maximum acceleration such that the cord will not break
  2. Find the time taken given the acceleration

It is given that the cord can handle a force up to 75.0 N. This means that the tension in the cord, caused by the water bucket's weight and the upward acceleration, cannot exceed 75.0 N. Hence,

\begin{split} F_{W, \mbox{bucket}} & = mg\\ & = (4.8)(9.8)\\ & = 47.04\ \mbox{N} \end{split} \begin{split} F_{\mbox{up}} & = ma_{\mbox{up}}\\ & = 4.8a_{\mbox{up}}\ \mbox{N} \end{split} \begin{split} F_{\mbox{max}} & = W_{\mbox{bucket}}+F_{\mbox{up}}\\ & = 47.04+4.8a_{\mbox{up}}\\ & = 75\ \mbox{N} \end{split}

Now, solve for $a_{\mbox{up}}$:

$$47.04+4.8a_{\mbox{up}}=75$$ $$4.8a_{\mbox{up}}=27.96$$ $$a_{\mbox{up}}=5.825\ ms^{-2}$$

This means that the maximum acceleration the cord can withstand is $5.825\ ms^{-2}$. To find the time it will take for the water bucket to be raised 12.0 m with the given acceleration, simply plug the numbers into the following equation:

$$s=ut+\frac{1}{2}at^2$$
$$12=0t+\frac{1}{2}(5.825)t^2$$ $$12=2.913t^2$$ $$4.120=t^2$$ $$2.030=t$$

From this, we know that the minimum time required for the water bucket to travel a vertical distance of 12 m without the cord breaking is 2.03 seconds.
