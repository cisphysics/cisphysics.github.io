Problem 4.35

Two horses pull horizontally on ropes attached to a stump. The two forces $\vec{F_1}$ and $\vec{F_2}$ that they apply to the stump are such that the net (resultant) force $\vec{R}$ equal to the magnitude of $\vec{F_1}$ and makes an angle of 90$^{\circ}$ with $\vec{F_1}$. Let $|\vec{F_1}|=1300$ N and $|\vec{R}|=1300$ N also. Find the magnitude of $\vec{F_2}$ and its direction (relative to $\vec{F_1}$).

Solution

Given the description of the problem, we know that $\vec{R}$ is the resultant force of $\vec{F_1}$ and $\vec{F_2}$. Writing this out mathematically, we get:

$$\vec{F_1}+\vec{F_2}=\vec{R}$$

In this case, $\vec{F_2}$ is the unknown. We know that both $\vec{F_1}$ and $\vec{R}$ have a magnitude of $1300\ N$ and are perpendicular to each other. Because they are perpendicular, we can assume $\vec{F_1}$ points 1300 to the right and $\vec{R}$ points 1300 upwards. We can then set $\vec{F_2}$ as the subject of the equation and write $\vec{F_1}$ and $\vec{R}$ in vector notation to get the following:

\begin{split} \vec{F_2} & = \vec{R}-\vec{F_1}\\ & = (0\hat{i}+1300\hat{j})-(1300\hat{i}+0\hat{j})\\ & = -1300\hat{i}+1300\hat{j} \end{split}

We can then find the magnitude of $\vec{F_2}$ using the vector magnitude formula:

$$|\vec{u}|=\sqrt{u_x^2+u_y^2}$$
\begin{split} |\vec{F_2}| & = \sqrt{(-1300)^2+(1300)^2}\\ & = 1300\sqrt{2}\ \mbox{N} \end{split}

Because the vector components are of equal magnitude, we also know the components form a 45-45-90 triangle. By visualizing the vectors, we know that $\vec{F_2}$ and $\vec{F_1}$ form a 135$^{\circ}$ angle.
